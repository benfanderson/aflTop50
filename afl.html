Dustin Martin

Richmond

He started the season bound for the same sort of impact he had in 2017 when he won the Brownlow and the Norm Smith medals and Richmond won the flag, but a knee problem slowed him considerably towards the end of the season and he barely kicked the ball in the preliminary final loss to Collingwood. But he still went at better than 25 disposals per game and he kicked 31 goals. When you sit down to compare “the big three” it is quality of disposal that gives “Dusty” the edge at the moment. He is an elite kick. It makes him that little bit more dangerous around goals and it makes him a bigger help to teammates in better scoring positions than him.
